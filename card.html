Arabian Horses

is a breed of horse that originated on the Arabian Peninsula. With a distinctive head shape and high tail carriage, the Arabian is one of the most easily recognizable horse breeds in the world.

  • : 800 to 1000 lbs
  • : 14.1 to 15.1 hands (57 to 61 inches, 145 to 155 cm)
  • : Bay, black, chestnut, or gray. Occasional dominant white, sabino, or rabicano patterns
  • : Finely chiseled bone structure, concave profile, arched neck, comparatively level croup, high-carried tail.

The Arabian developed in a desert climate and was prized by the nomadic Bedouin people, often being brought inside the family tent for shelter and protection from theft. Selective breeding for traits including an ability to form a cooperative relationship with humans created a horse breed that is good-natured, quick to learn, and willing to please.
